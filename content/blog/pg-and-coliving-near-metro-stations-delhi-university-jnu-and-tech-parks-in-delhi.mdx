## **Delhi's Metro Network: The Foundation of Housing Decisions**

Delhi's Metro Rail Corporation operates one of the world's largest urban Metro networks, with multiple lines spanning the National Capital Region. For housing decisions in Delhi, proximity to a Metro station and the specific line it is on should be the primary consideration after workplace location.

**Yellow Line (Line 2):** Connects Samaypur Badli in north Delhi to Huda City Centre in Gurgaon. This is Delhi's most important Metro corridor for professionals, passing through major residential and commercial areas including Chandni Chowk, New Delhi, Central Secretariat, Udyog Bhawan, Green Park, and AIIMS. For government and corporate professionals, Yellow Line connectivity is invaluable.

**Blue Line (Line 3/4):** The longest Metro line, connecting Dwarka Sector 21 in south-west Delhi to Noida Electronic City and Vaishali in Uttar Pradesh. Passes through Rajiv Chowk (the most important interchange station), Karol Bagh, Patel Nagar, Shadipur, and Janakpuri before crossing into Noida.

**Red Line (Line 1):** Connects Rithala in north-west Delhi to New Bus Addah in Ghaziabad. Important for residential areas in north Delhi including Rohini, Pitampura, and Rithala.

**Violet Line (Line 6):** Connects Kashmere Gate to Raja Nahar Singh in Faridabad, passing through central and south-east Delhi areas.

**Pink Line (Line 7):** The newest major line, connecting Majlis Park to Shiv Vihar and serving previously underserved areas including IP Extension, Trilokpuri, Mandawali, and East Vinod Nagar.

## **PG and Coliving Near Delhi University**

Delhi University (DU) is one of India's largest and most prestigious universities, with the main North Campus in Civil Lines and the South Campus in Dhaula Kuan. Each year, thousands of students from across India relocate to Delhi to attend DU and its affiliated colleges.

**North Campus area:** Neighbourhoods immediately adjacent to the North Campus — including Kamla Nagar, Patel Chest, GTB Nagar, Vishwavidyalaya, and Mukherjee Nagar — have historically been the primary student residential zones for DU North Campus students. These areas have a dense concentration of PG and student accommodation options.

The Yellow Line's Vishwavidyalaya station is immediately adjacent to the North Campus, making Metro connectivity excellent for students living in this area who need to access other parts of Delhi.

**South Campus area:** South Delhi neighbourhoods including Hauz Khas, Malviya Nagar, Vasant Vihar, and Munirka serve DU South Campus students. The Yellow Line's Hauz Khas and AIIMS stations provide connectivity from these areas.

**Competitive examination hubs:** Areas like Mukherjee Nagar and Rajender Nagar have developed as coaching and competitive examination hubs specifically serving UPSC aspirants. These areas have a very particular accommodation ecosystem serving students preparing for civil services examinations.

## **Coliving Near Delhi's Technology Parks and Corporate Hubs**

Delhi's technology sector is spread across multiple zones, with Noida and Gurgaon serving as the primary IT hubs. Within Delhi itself, technology companies and corporate offices cluster in specific areas.

**Nehru Place:** South Delhi's technology market and office hub, Nehru Place is one of the country's largest IT hardware markets and hosts numerous technology company offices. The Violet Line's Nehru Place station provides Metro connectivity.

**Okhla Industrial Area:** South Delhi's industrial zone hosts manufacturing, technology, and services companies. The Pink and Violet lines serve this area.

**Connaught Place and the Central Business District:** Delhi's historic commercial heart, Connaught Place hosts insurance companies, consulting firms, and major corporate offices. Yellow Line stations at Rajiv Chowk and Patel Chowk serve this area.

**Jasola Tech Park and surrounding areas:** South-east Delhi's growing technology zone, served by the Violet Line.

## **Practical Delhi Living Tips**

**Master the Metro map early:** Delhi's Metro system is extensive and initially complex. Invest time in the first week to understand the key interchange stations (Rajiv Chowk, Kashmere Gate, Central Secretariat, Hauz Khas) and how they connect your home station to your destination.

**Neighbourhood character varies enormously:** Delhi is not a monolithic city. South Delhi has a different character from North Delhi; West Delhi from Central Delhi. Understanding where you are in the city's social and cultural geography enriches daily life.

**Explore the cultural wealth:** Delhi has extraordinary cultural resources — the museums and monuments of central Delhi, the food culture of Chandni Chowk, the café scene of Hauz Khas, the weekend markets of Dilli Haat.
