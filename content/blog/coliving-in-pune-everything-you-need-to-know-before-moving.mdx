## **Why Pune Is a Major Hub for Coliving**

Pune's coliving market has grown rapidly over the last five years, driven by several converging forces. The city's IT sector, centred on Hinjewadi and Baner, employs hundreds of thousands of technology professionals. Its educational ecosystem, home to institutions like the University of Pune, Symbiosis, and dozens of engineering and management colleges, brings large numbers of students to the city each year.

Both groups share a common housing need: well-located, fully equipped, flexible, and community-oriented accommodation. Traditional PG housing serves neither group particularly well, which has created a significant market opportunity for coliving operators.

## **Best Neighbourhoods for Coliving in Pune**

Hinjewadi: Pune's primary IT hub, home to the Rajiv Gandhi Infotech Park, which houses offices of Infosys, Wipro, Tech Mahindra, and dozens of multinational technology companies. Professionals working in Hinjewadi prize accommodation in the surrounding area  Baner, Balewadi, and Wakad  to avoid the long commute from central Pune.

Baner: A rapidly developing residential and commercial area adjacent to Hinjewadi, Baner has excellent restaurants, cafes, and social infrastructure, making it one of Pune's most liveable neighbourhoods for young professionals.

Kothrud: A well-established, central neighbourhood known for its educational institutions, good connectivity, and relatively affordable accommodation. Popular with students and professionals alike.

Viman Nagar: Located near Pune International Airport, Viman Nagar is a vibrant, cosmopolitan neighbourhood with a young demographic and good connectivity to major employment zones.

Koregaon Park: Pune's most upscale residential neighbourhood, known for its tree-lined streets, high-end cafes, and international feel. Popular with senior professionals and entrepreneurs.

Aundh: A well-connected neighbourhood in north-west Pune, Aundh has good access to both Hinjewadi and central Pune, making it a convenient base for professionals working across multiple parts of the city.

Pune's student population is one of the largest in any Indian city outside Delhi and Mumbai. Students from across Maharashtra and from other states move to Pune each year for undergraduate, postgraduate, and professional degree programmes.

For students, coliving offers several specific advantages over traditional hostel or PG accommodation. The quality of the physical environment is typically much higher. Internet connectivity is reliable, which is essential for academic work and online learning. The community environment provides both social support and study partners.

Professionals working in Pune's technology sector are the other primary demographic for coliving spaces. The specific requirements of this group differ somewhat from students.

## **Tips for New Arrivals in Pune**

Get Your Location Right First: Before everything else, decide which part of Pune you need to be in based on your workplace or institution. Pune's traffic can be brutal, and being in the wrong part of the city significantly impacts daily life.

Use the Rainy Season as a Consideration: Pune receives significant monsoon rainfall between June and September. Some areas experience flooding and connectivity issues. Check property-specific experiences if you are moving during this period.

Explore the City: Pune has an outstanding restaurant and cafe scene, excellent cultural venues, and proximity to some of Maharashtra's best hiking and weekend destinations. A central, well-connected coliving space puts all of this within reach.
