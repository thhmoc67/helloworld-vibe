Kota, a mid-sized city on the banks of the Chambal river in Rajasthan, has an outsized reputation in India's education system. Every year, hundreds of thousands of students from across the country converge on Kota to attend the coaching institutes that have made it famous  primarily for IIT-JEE and NEET preparation. The city's entire economy, in many respects, is built around this influx of students. And at the centre of each student's daily experience is the question of where to live.

## **Kota's Student Housing Challenge**

The scale of student migration to Kota is extraordinary. At any given time, the city hosts somewhere between 150,000 and 200,000 students preparing for competitive entrance examinations. This creates an enormous demand for student accommodation that the traditional hostel and PG sector has historically struggled to meet in terms of quality, safety, and overall living experience.

Many students arrive in Kota from states across India  from Kerala, Tamil Nadu, West Bengal, Maharashtra, and beyond  often for the first time away from home. They are typically between 16 and 18 years old, academically motivated but socially and practically inexperienced. The quality of their accommodation directly affects their mental health, their academic performance, and their overall experience of one of the most challenging periods of their young lives.
