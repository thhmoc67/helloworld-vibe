## **Delhi's Housing Market: The Challenge**

Delhi's housing market is characterised by extreme diversity in quality and price. Luxury apartments in South Delhi and Gurgaon represent one end of the spectrum. Dense, lower-income neighbourhoods in East and North Delhi represent the other. For young professionals and students seeking something comfortable, affordable, and well-located in the middle, the market has historically been difficult to navigate.

Traditional PG accommodation in Delhi is abundant but highly variable in quality. Brokers are ubiquitous and expensive. Security deposits are often substantial. And the process of finding and vetting a suitable property can take weeks of frustrating effort.

## **Key Areas for Coliving in Delhi**

South Delhi: Neighbourhoods like Lajpat Nagar, Greater Kailash, and Malviya Nagar have historically been popular residential areas for professionals working in central Delhi. Good connectivity to the Cyber City area via the Metro makes South Delhi an attractive option.

West Delhi: Dwarka, Janakpuri, and Rajouri Garden are well-connected residential areas that offer relatively affordable accommodation while providing good Metro connectivity to workplaces across the city.

North Delhi: Areas like Rohini and Pitampura are major residential hubs in north Delhi, with good connectivity to the University of Delhi's north campus  making them popular with students.

Central Delhi: Proximity to Connaught Place, the government district, and major media and corporate offices makes central Delhi neighbourhoods like Karol Bagh and Rajendra Nagar perennially popular with working professionals.
